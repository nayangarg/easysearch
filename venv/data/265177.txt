-- T On Parallelization of Static Scheduling Algorithms . -- A AbstractMost static algorithms that schedule parallel programs represented by macro dataflow graphs are sequential . This paper discusses the essential issues pertaining to parallelization of static scheduling and presents two efficient parallel scheduling algorithms . two efficient parallel scheduling algorithms have been implemented on an Intel Paragon machine and two efficient parallel scheduling algorithms performances have been evaluated . two efficient parallel scheduling algorithms produce high-quality scheduling and are much faster than existing sequential and parallel algorithms . -- B Introduction Static scheduling utilizes the knowledge of problem characteristics to reach a global optimal , or near optimal , solution . Although many people have conducted many people research in various manners , they all share a similar underlying idea : take a directed acyclic graph representing the parallel program as input and schedule the parallel program onto processors of a target machine to minimize the completion time . This is an NP-complete problem in NP general form [ 7 ] . Therefore , many hueristic algorithms that produce satisfactory performance have been proposed [ 11 , 13 , 5 , 14 , 12 , 4 , 9 ] . Although two efficient parallel scheduling algorithms apply to parallel programs , two efficient parallel scheduling algorithmsare sequential , and are executed on a single processor system . A sequential algorithm is slow . Scalability of static scheduling is restricted since a large memory space is required to store the task graph . A natural solution to this problem is using multiprocessors to schedule tasks to multiprocessors . In fact , without parallelizing the scheduling algorithm and running the scheduling algorithm on a parallel computer , a scalable scheduler is not feasible . A parallel scheduling algorithm should have the following features : ffl High quality - it is able to minimize the completion time of a parallel program . ffl Low complexity - it is able to minimize the time for scheduling a parallel program . These two requirements contradict each other in general . Usually , a high-quality scheduling algorithm is of a high complexity . The Modified Critical-Path (Modified Critical-Path ) algorithm was introduced [ 13 ] , which offered a good quality with relatively low complexity . In this paper , we propose two parallelized versions of Modified Critical-Path . we will describe the Modified Critical-Path algorithm in the next section . Then , we will discuss different approaches for parallel scheduling , as well as existing parallel algorithms in section 3 . In sections 4 and 5 , we will present the VPMCP and HPMCP algorithms , respectively . In the following , the PEs that execute a parallel scheduling algorithm are called the physical PEs (physical PEs ) in order to distinguish them from the target PEs (target PEs ) to which the macro dataflow graph is to be scheduled . The BSA algorithm takes into account link contention and communication routing strategy . A processing element list is constructed in a breadth-first order from the processing element having the highest degree (pivot processing element ) . The BSA algorithm constructs a schedule incrementally by first injecting all the nodes to the pivot processing element . Then , The BSA algorithm tries to improve the start time of each node by migrating each node to the adjacent PEs of the pivot processing element only if the migration will improve the start time of the node . The complexity of the BSA algorithm is O (p 2 en ) , where p is the number of target PEs , n the number of nodes , and e the number of edges in a graph . The parallel BSA algorithm parallelizes the BSA algorithm in the horizontal scheme . The nodes in the graph are sorted in a topological order and partitioned into P equal sized blocks . After all the partitions are scheduled , the independently developed schedules are concatenated . Information estimation The major problem in the horizontal scheme is how to resolve the dependences between par- titions . In general , a latter partition depends on a latter partition former partitions . To schedule partitions in parallel , each PPE needs schedule information of each PPE . Since it is impossible to obtain such information before the schedules of former partitions have been produced , an estimation is necessary . Although the latter PPE does not know the exact schedules of the latter PPE former partitions , an estimation can help a node to determine a node earliest start time in the latter partition . In the parallel BSA algorithm [ 1 ] , the start time of each remote parent node (remote parent node ) is estimated . It is done by calculating two parameters : the earliest possible start time (earliest possible start time ) , and the latest possible start time (LPST ) . The earliest possible start time of a node is the largest sum of computation times from the start point to a node , excluding the node itself . The LPST of a node is the sum of computation times of all nodes scheduled before the node itself , excluding the node itself . Otherwise , it is equal to the length of the longest path from the start point through an remote parent node to the end point , divided by the length of the critical path . Parallel scheduling is a new approach and is still under development . Many open problems need to be solved . High-quality parallel scheduling algorithms with low complexity will be developed . High-quality parallel scheduling algorithms with low complexity can be achieved by parallelizing the existing sequential scheduling algorithms or by designing new parallel scheduling algorithms . We have developed the VPMCP and HPMCP algorithms by parallelizing the sequential Modified Critical-Path algorithm . Performance of this approach has been studied . Both VPMCP and HPMCP algorithms are much faster then parallel BSA .
