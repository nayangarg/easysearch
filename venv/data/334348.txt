-- T A Minimal Universal Test Set for Self-Test of EXOR-Sum-of-Products Circuits . -- A AbstractA testable EXOR-Sum-of-Products (EXOR-Sum-of-Products ) circuit realization and a simple , universal test set which detects all single stuck-at faults in the internal lines and the primary inputs/outputs of the realization are given . Since EXOR-Sum-of-Products is the most general form of AND-EXOR representations , our realization and test set are more versatile than those described by other researchers for the restricted Generalized Reed-Muller , Fixed Polarity Reed-Muller , and PPRM forms of AND-EXOR circuits . Our circuit realization requires only two extra inputs for controllability and one extra output for observability . The cardinality of our test set for an $ n $ input circuit is ($ n+6 $ ) . For Built-in Self-Test (Built-in Self-Test ) applications , we show that we test set can be generated internally as easily as a pseudorandom pattern and that our test set provides 100 percent single stuck-at fault coverage . In addition , our test set requires a much shorter test cycle than a comparable pseudoexhaustive or pseudorandom test set . -- B INTRODUCTION The large increase in the complexity of ASICs has led to a much greater need for circuit testability and Built-In-Self-Test (Built-in Self-Test ) [ 1 ] . The testability properties of different forms of two-level networks have attracted many researchers [ 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6 ] . The forms investigated include Positive Polarity Reed-Muller (PPRM ) [ 2 ] , Fixed Polarity Reed-Muller (Fixed Polarity Reed-Muller ) [ 6 ] , Generalized Reed-Muller (Generalized Reed-Muller ) [ 7 ] , and EXOR-Sum-of- Products (EXOR-Sum-of-Products ) [ 3 ] . All of the canonical Reed-Muller forms (PPRM , Fixed Polarity Reed-Muller , and Generalized Reed-Muller ) have restrictions on the allowed polarities of variables or on the allowed product terms . EXOR-Sum-of-Products , on the other hand , has no restrictions , and is formed by combining arbitrary product terms using EXORs [ 3 , 7 ] . Therefore , ESOPis the most general form of 2-level AND-EXOR networks . A traditional , signature analysis based Built-in Self Test (Built-in Self-Test ) circuitry for a combinational network consists mainly of a pattern generator , and a signature register . For the complete testing system , a Built-in Self-Test controller , some multiplexers , a comparator , and a ROM are also embedded inside the chip . Using a Linear Feedback Shift Register (Linear Feedback Shift Register ) -based PseudoExhaustive or Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator (Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator ) is a well-known method for generating test patterns for very large and complex combinational circuits . The Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator approach is used because The Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator approach is difficult to otherwise generate the large and irregular test sets required by such combinational circuits . As shown by Drechsler (et al . ) in [ 16 ] , the Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator approach does not work better with AND-EXOR circuits than the Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator approach does with equivalent SOP circuits . However , they show that AND-EXOR networks do have good deterministic testability performance . Considering this fact and the properties of our design , we can list the reasons why we propose a deterministic test generation for built-in self test of EXOR-Sum-of-Products circuits as below ; 1 . Our EXOR-Sum-of-Products realization is designed for testing and therefore requires a minimal test set . Traditional Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator test length is much longer than our test set for the same fault coverage . Also , there is no need for partitioning the circuit to prevent the long cycles of a pseudo-exhaustive test generation . 2 . Our test set is universal , which allows Our test set to be generated by fixed hardware that can be used for any function . 3 . Our test set has regular patterns and therefore easy to generate . As a result , the area overhead of Our pattern generator in Our scheme is comparable to that of Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator based schemes . 4 . Our test set gives 100 % fault coverage for single fault detection and does not require fault simulation . Figure 8 shows the Built-in Self-Test circuitry for EXOR-Sum-of-Products circuits . In this structure , the only difference from classical Built-in Self-Test circuitry is the EXOR-Sum-of-Products Deterministic Pattern Generator (ESOP Deterministic Pattern Generator ) that is introduced for our easily testable EXOR-Sum-of-Products implementation . The results from the applied test vectors are collected from the function output f and from the extra observable outputs then compressed in the signature register , which can simply be an Linear Feedback Shift Register based Multiple Input Signature Register (Multiple Input Signature Register ) [ 1 ] . After all the tests are applied , the signature register content is compared with the correct signature of the implemented EXOR-Sum-of-Products to generate a go/no go signal at the end of the test cycle . An EXOR cascade is used in the check part instead of AND gates or OR gates because the EXOR cascade yields much fewer test vectors and to- day 's advanced technology makes it possible to have an EXOR gate almost as fast as an AND gate . The experimental results show that our test set is always smaller in exponential degree than pseudo-random test set , and smaller in multiples than an algorithmically generated test set for 100 % single stuck-at fault coverage . A deterministic test pattern generator is presented to be used as a part of the built-in self-test circuitry . The experimental results show that the over-all overhead of our Built-in Self-Test circuit is comparable to that of the traditional Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator based Built-in Self-Test circuit . More importantly , our pattern generator is superior to a Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator because of a Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator 100 % single fault coverage and much shorter testing cycle . Also , a Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator does not require an initialization seed and the circuitry for generating a Pseudo-Random Pattern Generator . The experimental results also show that our 2-level EXOR-Sum-of-Products implementation is comparable to (or in some cases better than ) the multi-level and 2-level SOP implementations in The total Built-in Self-Test area and delay measurements . Furthermore , our implementation gives a very small DFT area overhead . In addition to detecting all single stuck-at faults , our architecture and test set detect a significant fraction of multiple stuck-at faults . More tests can be added to improve the multiple fault coverage even though it is very unlikely that a minimal and universal test set can be found . For instance , more zero- weighted test vectors can be added to improve the multiple fault coverage for the AND part of the circuit as explained by Saluja and Reddy in [ 12 ] . Another method to improve the fault coverage is to detect multiple faults with the help of multiple outputs in a multi-output EXOR-Sum-of-Products circuit . Our Built-in Self-Test methodology with an Multiple Input Signature Register is an ideal method for this purpose . Our test set can also be improved for bridging faults using a method similar to that presented by Bhattacharya (et al . ) in [ 27 ] . Our are currently investigating Our test set and implementation scheme for detecting bridging faults and multiple faults . The experimental results will be presented in Our next paper .
